01 · front of house

Takes orders end-to-end.

Customers order the way they actually talk — "yeah gimme two of the spicy tofu, no peanuts, and a Thai iced tea." Yellowfone captures items, modifiers, special requests, and customer details, confirms the order back, and books it.

  • Free-form speech, not menu trees
  • Modifiers, sides, allergies, special instructions
  • Reads phone numbers back digit-by-digit
  • Order pushes to Toast / Square / Clover automatically

02 · revenue

Upsells from your menu.

The reason great servers make great money is they ask. Yellowfone asks too — combos, sides, drinks, dessert, "want to add a side of …" — but only the moves you tell it to make, only when they fit.

  • Per-item upsell rules ("with pizza, suggest knots")
  • Combo offers ("make it a meal for $X more")
  • Quiet hours so it doesn't push at last call
  • Track upsell take-rate per item in the dashboard
03 · always on

Answers menu & hours questions.

Half of restaurant phone calls aren't orders — they're "are you open?" and "do you do gluten-free?" Yellowfone handles all of it from your real menu and hours, no scripts to maintain.

  • Hours, address, parking, dine-in vs takeout, holidays
  • Item descriptions, prices, sizes, prep time
  • "What's popular?" / "What's new?" — answered from your data
  • Confidently says "I don't know — let me grab someone" when it should

04 · safety

Handles allergens & substitutions.

Allergens are where wrong answers actually hurt people. Yellowfone is conservative — if there's any doubt, it says so. And it flags allergy orders for the kitchen so prep happens carefully.

  • Per-item allergen tags (gluten, dairy, nuts, shellfish, soy, egg)
  • Suggests safe alternatives when an item is off-limits
  • Flags allergy orders on the kitchen ticket
  • Honest about uncertainty — won't bluff a celiac question
05 · routing

Routes the call to the right person.

Some calls aren't for the bot. Catering for 200, complaints, vendors, the press — Yellowfone hands those off to the human you decide, with full context.

  • Catering inquiries → catering manager's mobile
  • Complaints → owner, with the call summary in a text
  • Vendors / cold sales → dropped to voicemail
  • "Speak to a human" always works, no maze

06 · multilingual

Speaks the language they speak.

Yellowfone detects the caller's language on the first sentence and switches. Your menu and rules don't need translating — it does that on the fly.

  • 30+ languages out of the box
  • Auto-detects from the caller's first words
  • Locks to your default language if you prefer
  • Mid-call switching when callers code-switch
07 · sync

SMS receipts. POS sync. Done.

The order doesn't end when the call ends. Yellowfone texts the customer a receipt with their order and ETA, and pushes the ticket to your POS so the kitchen sees it the same as any other order.

  • SMS receipt with order details + ETA
  • Native push to Toast, Square, and Clover
  • Customer can text back "cancel" or "running late"
  • Daily order recap email to the owner
